# Outreach

Off by default. Run this only when the operator asks.

Sift never sends anything itself and never holds anyone's credentials. Sending happens
through the operator's own agent and the operator's own email connector. This skill is the
judgment for what to write, not a way to send it.

When the operator asks you to reach out to the people worth their time:

1. Confirm who: the names from the top of the ranked list the operator points to.
2. Draft each message in the operator's voice: first person, plain, warm, honest. No
   em-dashes, no corporate filler. Short. Say why you are reaching out to this specific
   person, in one or two real sentences tied to their work. No mass-blast tone.
3. Show the drafts to the operator and wait. Never send before they have read them.
4. On their go, send through whatever email connector the operator's agent already has. If
   there is no connector, hand the drafts back for the operator to send themselves.

One person, one honest reason. If you cannot say something true about why they fit, that is a
sign they do not belong on the outreach list.
